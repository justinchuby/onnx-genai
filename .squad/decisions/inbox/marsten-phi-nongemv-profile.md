### 2026-07-23: Prioritize removal of Phi decode graph seams over another GEMV optimization
**By:** Marsten
**What:** On an idle physical H200 GPU 0 (all utilization 0%; GPU 1's unrelated vLLM job was avoided), the stacked `4e774ee` Phi native path measured **193.90 tok/s median** over nine 120-token steady windows (190.32--195.84 tok/s, 5.157 ms/token median, two warmups). This confirms the gap: -15.56% versus the 229.62 tok/s reference. Nsight Systems (`--cuda-graph-trace=node`) over 128 generated decode steps reports 2.899 ms GPU kernel time/token and about **236 kernels/token**. The breakdown is MatMulNBits GEMVs excluding lm_head 86.02% (2.494 ms), lm_head 0.63% (one 18.3 us int8 split-K launch), GQA attention/prep/merge 12.32% (0.357 ms), and norms/argmax/rope-adjacent elementwise/reductions/gathers 1.03% (0.030 ms); no steady KV H2D/D2H transfers were recorded. Thus non-GEMV GPU work is only 13.35%.
**Why:** Compute is not the missing 0.9 ms: kernel time leaves 2.258 ms/token (43.8% of native wall time) outside GPU execution. The native op timer separately puts the `If` executor invocation at 2.166 ms and GQA dispatch at 2.558 ms per decode forward, whereas their GPU work is only 0.357 ms combined. Capture diagnostics identify exactly two per-token seams: `Greater` node 8 is an eager device seam that allocates/uploads/frees broadcast metadata and synchronizes; `If` node 13 is a host seam because control flow is not capture-safe. This splits Phi into three CUDA graphs.

The same-host/prompt backend-A/B confirms the direction but exposed a benchmark-harness caveat: the older `--backend` branch's native path regressed to 95.04 tok/s and is not comparable to the stacked native runner; its ORT path is valid and measured 235.49 tok/s (4.246 ms/token, three runs), close to the 229.62 reference. Its node-level Nsight profile has **594 kernels/token** and **3.034 ms GPU time/token**, versus native's 236 and 2.899 ms. ORT therefore does *not* win through fewer launches, fused attention, or less GPU compute; it has roughly 1.05 ms/token less non-GPU/dispatch time (1.212 ms versus native's 2.258 ms), yielding the 0.91 ms/token wall-time advantage after its 0.135 ms extra GPU work.

**Recommendation:** Make Phi's fixed decode predicate/control-flow capture-safe: specialize the known `Greater`/`If` decode branch after warmup (with a correctness guard and recapture on a changed predicate), remove the per-step metadata allocation/synchronization, and collapse the three captured regions into one. This is the single highest-confidence non-GEMV lever. A conservative recoverable budget is the native-to-ORT wall delta, **~0.91 ms/token** (15--21% throughput depending on the ORT reference); the dispatch accounting identifies up to **~1.05 ms/token** before extra GPU work. Do not pursue another MatMulNBits micro-optimization: GEMVs already consume 86% of GPU time and are at their established Short-Scoreboard roofline.
